02 — Environmental Impact

What's Happening to Our Planet?

From melting glaciers to raging wildfires, ocean acidification to toxic air — the environmental crisis is no longer a distant forecast.

01 🌡️

Global Warming

Greenhouse gas emissions are driving temperatures higher, melting ice caps, raising sea levels, and fueling extreme weather. By 2050, global temperatures may rise 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels.

02 🌊

Ocean Ecosystem Crisis

The ocean absorbs roughly 30% of human CO₂ emissions, causing acidification. Coral reefs are bleaching and dying on a massive scale. Microplastics have infiltrated every level of the marine food chain. Over 90% of large fish populations have been overfished.

03 🌲

Deforestation

Every minute, we lose forest area equivalent to 27 football fields. The Amazon — Earth's lungs — is degrading at an alarming rate, threatening global carbon cycles and indigenous communities.

04 💨

Air Pollution

99% of the global population breathes air that exceeds safe limits. Around 7 million people die prematurely each year from pollution-related diseases — the single largest environmental health threat.

05 🐾

Mass Extinction

Species are vanishing at 1,000 times the natural background rate. From polar bears to bees, countless creatures are losing their habitats. Every extinction is an irreversible loss to the web of life.

06 💧

Freshwater Crisis

About 2.2 billion people worldwide lack access to safe drinking water. Industrial pollution, agricultural runoff, and climate change are making this crisis worse by the day.

03 — The Awakening

Milestones in Global Conservation

From the first Earth Day to the Paris Agreement, humanity has never stopped fighting for this planet.

1970
The First Earth Day
Twenty million Americans took to the streets demanding environmental protection, sparking the modern conservation movement. The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) was established that same year.
1987
Montreal Protocol
The world's first successful international treaty limiting ozone-depleting substances — hailed as a model of environmental diplomacy. The ozone layer has since begun to slowly recover.
1997
Kyoto Protocol
The first legally binding agreement to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, establishing the principle of "common but differentiated responsibilities" and launching the era of global carbon reduction.
2015
Paris Agreement
195 nations united to limit global warming to well below 2°C, pursuing efforts to stay within 1.5°C. A landmark moment in humanity's collective response to climate change.
2023
COP28 · Renewable Energy Pledge
At the Dubai climate summit, nations made the first explicit commitment to "transition away from fossil fuels," with a goal to triple global renewable energy capacity by 2030.
